Education should be for everyone.

Underserved children in resource-poor areas have the potential of becoming the next generation of innovators, engineers, and critical thinkers. Our goal is to tap into this potential and provide young students the opportunity to learn, through educative hands-on experiences.

The Agastya International Foundation, an educational charity based in India, challenged us to improve one of their devices.

The contraption was too large for kids and required a power outlet.

Meet the BrightBox.

Our current project, BrightBox, shines three rays of light onto a panel. Children can discover the laws of optics in a fun, interactive manner, by bending the light with mirrors and lenses.

We're a team of engineering students and faculty members.

At the Olin College of Engineering, engineering starts with people and ends with people. Hands On Learning applies that perspective to educating underserved children, and we aim to produce positive social change by sharing our passion for STEM: science, technology, engineering, and math.

Mission Statement
